Guns, Germs, and Steel by Jared Diamond



About the book
Why did some societies advance more quickly than others?
Jared Diamond delves into this complex question in Guns, Germs, and Steel, an engaging exploration of the forces that have shaped human history.
Drawing from biology, geography, and anthropology, Diamond argues that environmental factors, rather than inherent differences between peoples, have determined the development of civilizations.
This Pulitzer Prize-winning work offers a profound perspective on inequality and cultural evolution, making it a landmark in historical writing.


"The Silk Roads: A New History of the World" by Peter Frankopan



About the book
"The Silk Roads: A New History of the World" by Peter Frankopan redefines the way we view world history. Instead of focusing on Europe and the West, Frankopan highlights the central role of Asia and the interconnected network of trade routes known as the Silk Roads.
Spanning thousands of years, the book examines how these roads fostered the exchange of goods, ideas, cultures, and religions, shaping the rise and fall of empires. From the ancient Persian and Chinese civilizations to the globalized world of the 21st century, Frankopan tells the story of how these routes influenced events on a global scale.
Written with depth and clarity, this groundbreaking work offers a fresh perspective on how the East and West have historically depended on and influenced one another. It is a must-read for those who want to understand the world from a broader and more balanced perspective.
