'To Kill a Mockingbird' by Harper Lee



About the book
Harper Lee’s iconic novel is a compelling tale of morality and humanity, set in the racially segregated American South of the 1930s.
Through the innocent yet perceptive eyes of Scout Finch, readers are drawn into the struggles of her father, Atticus, a lawyer determined to uphold justice in the face of overwhelming prejudice.
Themes of compassion, courage, and integrity resonate throughout this masterpiece, making it an enduring favorite among readers and a cornerstone of modern literature.


'1984' by George Orwell



About the book
George Orwell’s 1984 portrays a terrifying dystopian future dominated by surveillance, propaganda, and authoritarian rule.
Winston Smith, the protagonist, struggles against the Party, a totalitarian regime led by the ominous Big Brother.
This haunting novel explores themes of freedom, truth, and individuality while serving as a sobering reminder of the consequences of unchecked political power.
Orwell’s striking vision continues to be a thought-provoking and chilling analysis of modern society.
